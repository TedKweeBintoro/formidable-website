Comprehensive Life Care Planning Services

Our certified life care planners provide detailed assessments and projections for individuals with complex medical needs

Catastrophic Injury Assessment

Comprehensive evaluation of individuals with severe injuries including spinal cord injuries, traumatic brain injuries, and complex orthopedic conditions to determine current and future care needs.

Medical Care Coordination

Development of coordinated care plans involving multiple medical specialties, therapies, and support services to optimize patient outcomes and quality of life.

Cost Projection Analysis

Detailed financial projections for lifetime medical costs, including equipment, medications, therapies, and attendant care services based on current medical standards and regional pricing.

Equipment & Technology Assessment

Evaluation of assistive technology, medical equipment, and adaptive devices needed for optimal function and independence, including replacement schedules and costs.

Home Modification Planning

Assessment of residential modifications needed for accessibility, safety, and independence, including detailed specifications and cost analysis for adaptation projects.

Expert Testimony Services

Professional testimony regarding life care planning methodologies, medical necessity of recommended services, and cost projections for litigation and settlement proceedings.

Our Life Care Planning Process

We follow evidence-based methodologies and industry standards to develop comprehensive, defensible life care plans

1

Medical Record Review

Comprehensive analysis of medical history, current conditions, and prognosis from treating physicians and specialists

2

Clinical Assessment

Direct evaluation of functional capacity, medical needs, and quality of life factors through professional assessment

3

Care Plan Development

Creation of detailed care plans addressing medical, therapeutic, equipment, and support service needs over the individual's lifetime

4

Cost Analysis

Detailed financial projections based on current regional costs, inflation factors, and evidence-based utilization patterns
